# Scoutpost FAQ in text form

## Licensing

Scoutpost is source-available under the Sustainable Use License. Newsrooms can use, modify, and deploy it internally. You cannot resell it, host it as a competing service, or white-label it as your own hosted product.

## Self-hosting

Self-hosting is supported. You bring your own Supabase, Gemini, Firecrawl, Resend, Apify, and hosting setup. The public setup skill is:

- https://www.scoutpost.ai/skills/scoutpost-setup.md

## Hosted vs self-hosted

- **Hosted**: sign in at scoutpost.ai and use the managed product with credits.
- **Self-hosted**: deploy it yourself, keep full control, and use your own API keys.

## Product basics

Scoutpost, formerly coJournalist, helps journalists monitor beats, sources, pages, social profiles, and civic sites through scheduled scouts. Agents can help search, summarize, and export the resulting information units, but journalists remain responsible for verification.

## Open source

Scoutpost is built on community-maintained open-source projects: Crawl4AI (primary scraper), Playwright (browser automation), Poppler/pdftotext (civic-PDF extraction), pgvector (semantic scout search), langdetect (dedup language scoring), the Internet Archive Wayback Machine (optional evidence snapshots), and satellite.js (Transport Scout orbital math). Full credits: https://www.scoutpost.ai/acknowledgements
